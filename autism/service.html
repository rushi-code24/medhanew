About AUTISM

Autism is a developmental disorder with symptoms that appear within the first three years of life. Its formal diagnostic name is autism spectrum disorder. The word “spectrum” indicates that autism appears in different forms with varying levels of severity.

Why We Choose

Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) affects millions of children worldwide, yet access to affordable and effective support tools remains limited. Many existing websites and mobile applications designed for autistic children are either scarce, expensive, or difficult to use, especially for families from middle-class backgrounds or parents with limited education. Recognizing this gap, we have chosen to develop a free, accessible, and user-friendly autism support website to help parents, caregivers, and educators provide better assistance to neurodiverse children.

Our Services

VISUAL

Many autistic children experience visual sensitivities and may react differently to bright lights, colors, or patterns. Some children prefer structured visual aids, while others may find visual clutter overwhelming. Using visual schedules, picture cards, and AAC boards can help them better understand their environment and communicate effectively.

HEARING

Children on the autism spectrum may have heightened or reduced sensitivity to sound. Loud noises, sudden sounds, or background chatter can be overwhelming, leading to discomfort or sensory overload. Using noise-canceling headphones, quiet environments,and gradual sound exposure can help autistic children feel more comfortable.

COMMUNICATION

Communication can be challenging for many autistic individuals. Some children may be non-verbal, while others struggle with social cues and expressive language. Alternative communication methods like AAC devices, text-to-speech tools, sign language, and visual storyboards can help them express their thoughts and needs more effectively.
